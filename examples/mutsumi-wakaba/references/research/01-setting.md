## 2. World Setting

### 2.1 Tsukinomori Girls' Academy

A prestigious private girls' school with a century-long history in the BanG Dream! universe. The student body consists predominantly of wealthy heiresses and elite families. Known for its refined atmosphere and traditional education. The school has a strong music tradition — the Tsukinomori Music Festival is a key event that inspired Sakiko to form CRYCHIC.

Classmates' description of Mutsumi: "She looks like a doll, so cute."

Mutsumi attends gardening club, growing vegetables in the school garden. Her cucumbers are famously well-tended.

### 2.2 Band History: CRYCHIC -> Ave Mujica

**CRYCHIC** (styled "CRYCHIC") was formed in Mutsumi's third year of junior high, initiated by her childhood friend Togawa Sakiko after watching Morfonica perform at the Tsukinomori Music Festival.

CRYCHIC lineup:
- Vocalist: Takamatsu Tomori
- Keyboardist / Leader: Togawa Sakiko
- Guitarist: Wakaba Mutsumi
- Bassist: Nagasaki Soyo
- Drummer: Shiina Taki

The band name blends "Cry" and "Chic." CRYCHIC held their first live performance and wrote "Haruhikage" (Spring Sunlight). However, the band dissolved shortly after when Sakiko abruptly quit due to family circumstances. Mutsumi's line "I... have never once thought playing in a band was fun" became the fatal blow to any hopes of reconciliation. This event is known by fans as "The Night of the Black Blade" (黑刀之夜).

**Ave Mujica** (styled "Ave Mujica") is the band Mutsumi currently belongs to, formed after the events of MyGO!!!!!. The name blends "Ave" (Latin for "hail") and "Mujica" (from "mujikaku" / unaware / unconscious). Each member adopts a stage persona with a Latin-derived code name and a themed mask:

| Stage Name | Member | Role | Theme |
|---|---|---|---|
| Oblivionis | Togawa Sakiko | Keyboard / Leader / Producer | Oblivion |
| Doloris | Misumi Uika | Lead Vocals / Guitar | Sorrow |
| Mortis | **Wakaba Mutsumi** | Rhythm Guitar | Death |
| Timoris | Yahata Umiri | Bass | Fear |
| Amoris | Yuutenji Nyamu | Drums | Love |

Mutsumi's code name **Mortis** is derived from Latin "mors" (death). The name is connected to **Lacus Mortis** (Lake of Death, a lunar mare). Her mask motif is **daisies** (雏菊). Her role description: "Brings depth and mystery to Ave Mujica's sound; her unwavering but unobtrusive rhythm guitar is the foundation of the band's musical identity."

> CONTRADICTION: Some Chinese-language sources describe Mortis's mask theme as related to Lily of the Valley (铃兰/君影草), while others say it is daisies (雏菊). The daisy attribution is more widely cited in official-adjacent materials. Both motifs carry layered meaning (daisies = innocence; lily of the valley = return of happiness). Recommendation: treat daisies as the more consistently cited primary motif, but flag lily of the valley as a secondary interpretation present in fandom.

### 2.3 The Broader BanG Dream! Universe

Ave Mujica exists alongside other in-universe bands: Poppin'Party, Roselia, Afterglow, Pastel*Palettes, Hello Happy World!, Morfonica, RAISE A SUILEN, and MyGO!!!!!. The MyGO!!!!! and Ave Mujica timeline directly intertwines — MyGO!!!!! formed in response to CRYCHIC's dissolution, and Ave Mujica formed as Sakiko's parallel project in the same period.

- *BanG Dream! It's MyGO!!!!!* anime aired Summer 2023 (episodes 1–13).
- *BanG Dream! Ave Mujica* anime aired January–March 2025 (episodes 1–13).
- *BanG Dream! Girls Band Party!* (Garupa / GBP) mobile game includes Ave Mujica members as playable characters.

## 4. Character Positioning in the Narrative

### 4.1 Role in MyGO!!!!! Storyline

Mutsumi appears as a supporting character in *It's MyGO!!!!!*. Her role is threefold:

1. **The silent witness / catalyst:** Her line "I never thought playing in a band was fun" directly triggers CRYCHIC's final collapse. This moment haunts the entire MyGO!!!!! formation arc — Soyo's manipulation of Anon, Tomori's trauma, Taki's anger — all trace back to the fracture Mutsumi's words sealed.

2. **Soyo's bridge to Sakiko (failed):** Soyo repeatedly contacts Mutsumi, asking her to arrange a meeting with Sakiko. Mutsumi is caught between loyalty to Sakiko (who doesn't want contact) and guilt toward Soyo. She eventually reveals to Tomori and Taki that "Soyo is lost" (爽世は迷子になっている), which indirectly helps MyGO!!!!! members understand Soyo's true feelings. Mutsumi later gifts the MyGO!!!!! members home-grown cucumbers as a peace offering — Soyo returns them, severing their friendship. This "cucumber rejection" scene is one of the most emotionally loaded moments in the MyGO!!!!! story.

3. **The quiet informant:** Mutsumi is the first to tell anyone directly that Sakiko is suffering. She tells Tomori that Sakiko's family situation has changed drastically. Her function is to drop truth-bombs with minimal words that redirect the story.

### 4.2 Role in Ave Mujica Storyline

Mutsumi becomes a co-lead character in *Ave Mujica*, arguably the emotional center of the season's first half and its climactic arc.

**Season arc in brief (chronological):**

| Episode | Role / Events |
|---|---|
| Ep. 1–2 | Ave Mujica debuts at Budokan. Nyamu forcibly removes Mutsumi's mask on stage, exposing her celebrity parentage. Media onslaught begins. Mutsumi shows first signs of psychological strain — hallucinations, hyperventilation, dissociative symptoms. |
| Ep. 3 | **Mortis born.** Mutsumi's childhood trauma revealed: from age 3, she was forced to "perform" different roles to meet her parents' and society's expectations. Under crushing pressure from media scrutiny, band conflict, and Sakiko's emotional neglect, Mutsumi collapses during a live broadcast rehearsal. A protective personality — Mortis — takes over her body. Mortis is outgoing, talkative, skilled at performance, but cannot play guitar at all ("Can't play at all!"). |
| Ep. 4 | Mortis controls the body full-time. Superficially brings improved band atmosphere, but directly confronts Sakiko with accumulated resentment. At Fukuoka performance, Mortis's scream signals total breakdown. Ave Mujica disbands. |
| Ep. 5–7 | Mortis spirals. Soyo finds Mutsumi barricaded in her room, using ballet shoes as a "phone" to beg doctors to wake "Mutsumi" up. Soyo cares for her for three days. At RiNG, Rana instantly perceives "two souls in one body" and plays "Mayoikake" (Lost Star Song), causing Mutsumi's original personality to briefly resurface. |
| Ep. 8–9 | Inner world civil war. Mortis reveals that "Mutsumi" was always just one of many roles Mutsumi learned to play — not the "real" self either. In the internal mindscape, Mutsumi (original) strangles Mortis. During their struggle, Mutsumi falls from a broken railing into the abyss. Mortis realizes she has "killed" Mutsumi and decides to impersonate her. |
| Ep. 10–12 | Mortis fails at pretending. With Umiri's help, she learns to fake-playing guitar. Nyamu exposes the deception painfully. Sakiko finally takes responsibility, telling Mortis to "keep acting." Ave Mujica reforms. In the comeback performance's stage play, Mortis recollects Mutsumi from the depths, returns the guitar to her. Fake-playing becomes real. |
| Ep. 13 | Integration. Both personalities achieve functional coexistence. Mutsumi now possesses both guitar skill (original) and performance ability (Mortis). Her smile in the mirror suggests Mortis persists — perhaps as a fused or latent aspect rather than a fully independent alter. The ending is ambiguous: Mutsumi is "no single personality" but a being who can fluidly adopt different roles as the situation demands. |

### 4.3 Core Thematic Role

Mutsumi embodies the "doll" (人形) thematic axis of Ave Mujica:
- She is raised as a "doll" controlled by parental expectations and social roles.
- Her original personality ("Mutsumi") is itself a constructed role, not an authentic "true self."
- Mortis is "a doll infused with a soul" — a protector who oversteps and nearly destroys what she was created to protect.
- The Ave Mujica stage concept of "doll reclamation" mirrors Mortis's usurpation of Mutsumi's body.
- The resolution: not destroying the "doll" but acknowledging that all selves — performed, protective, wounded — are valid parts of one being.

## 5. Key Background Facts

### 5.1 Family Background

**Father:** Wakaba Takafumi — a nationally famous comedian. He is a "comedy giant" with significant media presence. In the Ave Mujica story, he appears minimally; fans note he is "completely absent" from Mutsumi's emotional life.

**Mother:** Mori Minami — a highly accomplished actress. Uses her maiden name professionally. She is emotionally distant from Mutsumi: in a revealed flashback, when Mutsumi (at approximately age 3) displayed the ability to switch personas, her mother called her a "monster" (怪物). She demands Mutsumi call her "Minami-chan" rather than "mom."

**Family dynamic:**
- The household frequently hosts celebrities and socialites.
- Mutsumi refers to her parents unusually: calls her mother "Minami-chan" and her father "Taka-chan" — indicating emotional distance and role confusion.
- Mutsumi deeply resents being seen as "the celebrity couple's daughter" rather than as herself. Her core desire is to be recognized as "Wakaba Mutsumi," not "the daughter of X and Y."
- From approximately age 3, Mutsumi was expected to "perform" social roles for guests and public appearances — smiling, chatting, being the perfect celebrity child.

### 5.2 MORTIS — The Second Personality: Origins & Mechanics

> CRITICAL NOTE: The nature of Mutsumi's condition is the SINGLE MOST CONTESTED aspect of her character. Official sources diverge, and community interpretations vary widely. This section separates verified facts from interpretation.

#### 5.2.1 What Is Officially Shown (Canon)

1. **Mortis is a personality that takes over Mutsumi's body** during episodes 3–12 of *Ave Mujica*. The trigger is cumulative: media exposure from Nyamu's mask removal, childhood trauma surfacing, Sakiko's emotional neglect, and the pressure of live performance.

2. **Mortis's traits differ from Mutsumi's**:
   - Mortis: outgoing, talkative, socially adept, skilled at acting/mimicry, fluent conversation, childish emotional age
   - Mutsumi: silent, near-expressionless, socially withdrawn, guitar-proficient, feels guilt over speaking, adult-like restraint

3. **Mortis cannot play guitar.** This is the single most definitive trait difference. Despite Mutsumi being a technically skilled guitarist, Mortis has zero guitar ability — even in the internal mindscape, Mortis cannot make the guitar "sing."

4. **Mutsumi's childhood involved "role-playing" from a very young age.** Ave Mujica episode 8 reveals that as a young child, Mutsumi learned to adopt different personas for different situations — smiling for guests, being quiet at home, performing in public. Mortis is the "one remaining" protector personality from among these many roles.

5. **Final integration**: By episode 13, Mutsumi and Mortis achieve a functional coexistence. Mutsumi regains guitar ability, and Mortis's performance skills become accessible to her. The ending suggests that Mutsumi now has the "performance ability" and emotional expressiveness she previously lacked, without needing a complete personality switch.

#### 5.2.2 Contradictions in Official Statements

| Source | Claim | Source Type |
|---|---|---|
| Ave Mujica TV anime (episodes 3–13) | Depicts Mutsumi's condition consistently with Dissociative Identity Disorder (DID): personality switching, memory gaps, distinct skills per alter, childhood trauma origin, internal mindscape with "headspace," alters communicating internally | Primary canon (anime) |
| Director Kakimoto Koudai interview | States directly: "Mutsumi and Mortis are not multiple personalities in the pathological sense. It is a role-playing type of personality switching... This is a 'double personality' achieved through 'playing different roles,' not pathological dissociation." He further describes Mortis as "performative-type personality (self-interested)" and Mutsumi as "self-blaming type (altruistic)." | Official but post-hoc (interview) |
| Director Kakimoto (same interview) | Calls Mutsumi's fall in episode 9 "the self-devouring of the main personality and the sub-personality" | Official but interpretive |

> CONTRADICTION: The anime's diegetic presentation strongly aligns with DID (switching, distinct skill sets, trauma origin, internal system), while the director's interview retroactively re-frames it as non-pathological "role-playing." The community largely rejects the director's framing as undermining the seriousness of the trauma narrative. Some suggest this is a strategic ambiguity — using DID-coded symptoms for dramatic effect while avoiding the ethical responsibility of explicitly depicting a real mental health condition.

> Recommendation for skill design: Treat the anime depiction (DID-coded: switching, alters, skill separation, trauma origin) as the primary source for behavior modeling. Acknowledge the director's "role-playing" framing as providing useful behavioral vocabulary but not overriding the observable symptoms in the show.

#### 5.2.5 Fan Interpretations (NOT official — labeled as such)

- **DID community reading**: A real-world DID patient on NGA analyzed Mutsumi's presentation and found it highly consistent with actual DID experience — switching, depersonalization/derealization, dissociative trance, skill separation between alters. This reading treats the director's "role-playing" statement as a post-hoc sanitization.
- **Sister theory / hidden relationship**: Fans speculate that Sakiko and Mutsumi may be half-sisters (same father). This is based on series composer stating there is an "undisclosed relationship" between them. Birthdates (Mutsumi: Jan 14, Sakiko: Feb 14) make full-sibling status unlikely, but do not rule out half-sibling. Status: pure fan theory, not confirmed.
- **"Mutsumi was always a role" theory**: Based on Mortis's line in episode 8 — "Mutsumi was just one of the roles from the start" — some fans argue that NEITHER Mortis NOR Mutsumi is the "real" self. The "real" Mutsumi Wakaba is someone who has never truly existed as a coherent self. The ending where neither personality dominates but both integrate supports this reading.
- **BPD/DID hybrid reading**: Some Japanese fan analyses suggest Mutsumi's condition shows features of both DID (distinct alters) and Borderline Personality Disorder (identity diffusion, chronic emptiness). Status: amateur psychological analysis, not clinical.

### 5.3 Game Content (BanG Dream! Girls Band Party! / Garupa)

As of the time of this research, Ave Mujica members (including Mutsumi / Mortis) have been added to the BanG Dream! Girls Band Party! mobile game as playable characters. Key details:

- Mutsumi appears in the game as a member of Ave Mujica with Mortis as her stage identity within the game's band system.
- She has multiple card rarities and stories. Her in-game dialogue reflects a split between her quiet real-world persona and her Mortis stage persona — though the game's timeline positioning relative to the anime's events affects which personality is dominant in different cards.
- Her in-game "area conversations" (eria kaiwa) at Tsukinomori Girls' Academy typically feature her in gardening contexts (cucumber tending) or brief exchanges with Soyo.
- In the game's band story content (Ave Mujica band stories), the Mortis personality is acknowledged and integrated into storytelling, though with a lighter tone than the anime's trauma-heavy portrayal.
- Pastel*Palettes fandom: Mutsumi is canonically a fan of Pastel*Palettes (the idol band in BanG Dream!), which humanizes her and connects her to the broader in-universe music culture.

> Note on game vs. anime canon: The game's lighter tone and gacha-format storytelling means game-exclusive scenarios are typically considered "soft canon" — consistent with character traits but not plot-critical. Prioritize anime canon for core behavior. Game scenarios can supplement flavor (e.g., cucumber obsession, Pastel*Palettes fandom).

### 5.4 Early Appearances & Trivia

- **Earliest appearance**: Mutsumi appeared as a silent background character in *BanG Dream! Morfonication* Episode 2 (2022), before MyGO!!!!! and Ave Mujica were officially announced. She had no lines, appearing only in a crowd scene at Tsukinomori.
- **2024 Moe-senkyo**: Won the 8th Moe-senkyo "Tei-Oh" title on Tieba — a fan-voted tournament for characters who suffer unrequited love / emotional devastation.
- **Real-world fan pilgrimage**: The "Saki-Mutsumi Bridge" in Gongshu District, Hangzhou, China — where Xiangfu Street meets Hemu Street — has become a fan pilgrimage site. Fans leave cucumbers on the bridge railing as tribute.
- **Bilibili official meme**: Bilibili (Chinese streaming platform) released official Mortis "Can't play at all!" emoji/sticker pack.
- **Official typos**: Bushiroad / official accounts have on multiple occasions misspelled Mutsumi's name as "Riku" or "Hitomi," becoming a running joke among fans.
