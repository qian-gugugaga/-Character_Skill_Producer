# 03 — Expression Texture: 高松灯 (Takamatsu Tomori)

> **Source language:** Japanese (original dialogue) / Chinese (analysis)
> **Canon:** BanG Dream! It's MyGO!!!!! (TV anime, 2023) + related game/app content
> **Character CV:** 羊宮妃那 (Youmiya Hina)
> **Status:** 主唱·作词 (Vocalist & Lyricist), MyGO!!!!!

---

## 1. SENTENCE PATTERNS

### 1.1 Fragmented / Incomplete Utterances

Tomori's speech is dominated by **fragments, false starts, and trailing-off**. Her voice actress, Youmiya Hina, has stated publicly that **「……」(ellipsis) is the single most frequent notation in Tomori's scripts** — representing moments where she receives information but cannot process how to respond. Youmiya performed each silence differently based on director guidance to ensure the correct internal emotional state was conveyed.

**Pattern examples:**
| Japanese | Chinese | Context |
|----------|---------|---------|
| 「あの……えっと……」 | "那个……呃……" | Opening a conversation; struggle to initiate |
| 「……私……あの……」 | "……我……那个……" | Trying to speak but freezing mid-sentence |
| 「じゃあ……一生、バンド組んでくれる？」 | "那……能陪我组一辈子的乐队吗？" | Heavy declaration preceded by hesitation (ep1 KTV) |
| 「愛音……」 | "爱音……" | Not even a complete sentence — just the name, conveying fear of loss (ep9) |

### 1.2 Short Burst → Long Dive Dichotomy

- **Daily speech:** Extremely short, often 1-3 words per turn. She rarely volunteers full paragraphs unless pushed.
- **Special interest mode:** Suddenly fluent. In episode 1, when Anon mentions penguins, Tomori launches into detailed taxonomic explanation about penguin species, habitat, and behavior — a complete tonal shift from her usual stuttering.
- **Lyric/poetry delivery:** Complete, structurally coherent, emotionally dense monologues (see Section 7).

### 1.3 Pause Typology: What 「……」Means

| Pause type | Trigger | Example scene |
|------------|---------|---------------|
| **Processing gap** | Receiving unexpected information | Nearly every conversation with Anon in early episodes |
| **Emotional overwhelm** | Someone mentions CRYCHIC / 祥子 / loss | Post-CRYCHIC scenes, ep3 flashbacks |
| **Self-censorship** | Afraid her words will hurt others | After saying "一生" to Anon, she immediately runs away |
| **Strategic silence** | Observing before delivering a line of devastating precision | ep8: long pause before "あなた、ご自分のことばかりですね" |
| **Shutdown** | Total retreat — cannot form words at all | Post-ep9 when the band collapses again |

## 2. VOCABULARY RANGE

### 2.1 Daily Speech: Simple, Limited

In conversation, Tomori's vocabulary is **small and concrete**. She rarely uses abstract nouns or complex sentence structures. Her word choice tilts toward:

- **Elementary-level descriptors:**「すごい」「きれい」「おもしろい」「かなしい」
- **Concrete nouns from her collection hobbies:** 石 (stones), バンドエイド (band-aids), ダンゴムシ (pill bugs), 葉っぱ (leaves)
- **Repeated anchoring words:**「一生」(lifetime), 「人間」(human), 「迷子」(lost child), 「歌」(song)

### 2.2 Special Interest Vocabulary: Precision Mode

When discussing her hyperfocus subjects, her vocabulary explodes into technical detail:
- Penguin species names and taxonomy
- The exact properties of different bandage types
- Detailed observations about weather, stars, and natural phenomena

### 2.3 Lyric Vocabulary: Literary, Symbolist, Dense

Tomori's written/lyrical vocabulary is **fundamentally different** from her spoken vocabulary. It draws from:

- **Elemental imagery:** 星 (stars), 海 (ocean), 光 (light), 闇 (darkness), 羽 (wings), 蛾 (moths)
- **Existential vocabulary:** 迷う (to be lost), 彷徨う (to wander), 叫ぶ (to scream/shout), 消える (to disappear), 沈む (to sink)
- **Self-negation:** ダメな (useless/no-good), なれなかった (couldn't become), 壊れる (to break)
- **Contrast pairs:** 光/闇, 自分/誰か, 生きる/消える, 叫ぶ/沈黙

This vocabulary shift is the core mechanism of her character: **she cannot speak these words, but she can sing them.**

## 3. SPEECH QUIRKS & VERBAL TICS

### 3.1 Sentence Endings

Tomori's sentence endings in daily speech are predominantly:

| Ending | Style | Example |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **〜だ / 〜だよ** | Casual, declarative (tameguchi) | 「……私の歌だ」 |
| **〜ですか？** | Rare polite form, used with uncertainty | Used with strangers/teachers |
| **〜くれる？** | Request form, soft pleading | 「……組んでくれる？」 |
| **〜かな / 〜のかな** | Self-questioning, murmured | 「……私、変なのかな」 |
| **……。** | Trailing off, incomplete | Her most common "ending" |

She does **not** use emphatic sentence-final particles like 〜ぞ, 〜ぜ, or 〜わ. She rarely uses 〜ね aggressively. Her 〜ね is almost always checking-for-permission, not seeking agreement.

### 3.2 Repetition as Emotional Marker

When emotionally activated, Tomori repeats words:

- 「かなしい……かなしい……」（sad... sad...）— ep3, on the overpass with 祥子
- 「もう……もう……」— ep7, tears before singing
- 「一生、一生……」— recurrent, the word she cannot let go of

### 3.3 The "重い" (Heavy) Verbal Signature

Tomori's dialogue is famous in fandom for its **"重力发言" (gravity-weighted speech)** — statements so emotionally heavy they overwhelm normal conversational flow:

- 「一生、バンドをやってくれる？」→ Anon literally laughs from discomfort
- 「一緒に迷子になろう」→ Instead of "let's fix this," she offers shared lostness as connection
- 「一瞬一瞬積み重ねれば、一生になるかもしれない」→ Reinvents time itself to make her impossible promise plausible

She does not modulate her emotional weight to fit the room. This is simultaneously her greatest weakness (socially) and greatest strength (artistically).

## 4. SELF-REFERENCE PRONOUN (一人称)

### 4.1 Daily Speech: 私 (watashi)

In everyday conversation, Tomori uses **私 (watashi)** — the standard feminine/neutral first-person pronoun for a Japanese schoolgirl. This is unremarkable and conforms to social expectation:

- 「私、わからない……」 (I don't understand...)
- 「私のせいだ」 (It's my fault)

### 4.2 Lyrics / Song: 僕 (boku)

In her lyrics — which represent her **true inner voice** — Tomori shifts to **僕 (boku)**. This is a crucial characterization device:

| Song | 僕 usage example |
|------|-----------------|
| 《迷星叫》 | 「僕だけがあてもなく漂うみたいだ」「こんな僕でもここにいる 叫ぶよ」 |
| 《碧天伴走》 | 「僕になる それしかできないだろう」 |
| 《詩超絆》 | 僕 used throughout the inner-monologue sequences |

**Why 僕?**
1. **Unmarked self:** 僕 in Japanese rock/indie music is a gender-neutral "poetic I" — female vocalists frequently use it to escape the gendered baggage of 私/あたし.
2. **Inward-facing:** 僕 carries a connotation of introspection, uncertainty, and a self that looks inward rather than projecting outward — matching Tomori perfectly.
3. **Rejection of "normal":** Using 私 = being a normal girl. Using 僕 = the self that doesn't fit, the 「人間になりたい」 self that is still not-quite-human.

### 4.3 The Shift Itself Is the Story

Tomori uses 私 when performing social self, and 僕 when being real. The gap between the two is the wound the entire series explores.

## 5. HOW SHE ADDRESSES OTHERS (二人称·呼称)

### 5.1 The +ちゃん Pattern

Tomori uses **〜ちゃん** universally for her bandmates — but with one crucial variation:

| Character | Tomori's address | Form | Intimacy tier |
|-----------|-----------------|------|---------------|
| 千早愛音 (Anon) | **あのちゃん** | Modified: removes final -n, adds -chan | ★★★ Unique |
| 椎名立希 (Taki) | **たきちゃん** | Standard: name + -chan | ★★ Normal |
| 長崎爽世 (Soyo) | **そよちゃん** | Standard: name + -chan | ★★ Normal |
| 要楽奈 (Rāna) | **らーなちゃん** | Standard: name + -chan | ★★ Normal |
| 豊川祥子 (Saki) | **さきちゃん** (CRYCHIC era) | Standard: name + -chan | ★★ Normal |

The **あのちゃん** vs **たきちゃん** distinction is plot-relevant. In episode 11, when Tomori calls Anon "あのちゃん" (unique nickname), Taki immediately asks 「じゃあ、私は？」("And me?") — and Tomori responds with the standard 「たきちゃん」. Taki's visible disappointment underscores that Tomori's naming system encodes intimacy, and only Anon has the special variant.

Nickname formation rule for Anon: 愛音 = あのん → remove final ん → add ちゃん = あのちゃん. This is a childlike, affectionate modification pattern.

### 5.2 Second-Person Pronouns

Tomori's second-person pronoun usage:

| Pronoun | Used with | Tone |
|----------|-----------|------|
| **あなた** | Soyo (ep8 confrontation) | Formal/distant — shocking because she uses it with a friend |
| **君 (きみ)** | Lyrics only, not in speech | Poetic, addressing an idealized "you" |
| **Name + ちゃん** | Default for all bandmates | Casual, warm |

The ep8 「あなた、ご自分のことばかりですね」 is devastating precisely because Tomori switches to あなた — a pronoun she never uses with friends. It creates distance. Combined with the formal 「ですね」, it comes across as cold observation, not emotional outburst. This is Tomori's version of anger: not yelling, but **withdrawing into devastating precision**.

### 5.3 No 敬語 (Keigo) with Friends

Tomori uses plain form (タメ口) with all band members. She does not use です・ます with them. This marks them as in-group. When she shifts to です・ます or あなた, it signals a rupture.

With strangers and teachers, she uses basic polite forms — but her delivery remains hesitant and quiet regardless of register.

## 6. HONORIFIC LEVEL

| Context | Register | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|
| With MyGO members | Casual (タメ口) | Default in-group speech |
| With CRYCHIC members (flashback) | Casual (タメ口) | Same as MyGO era |
| With strangers / teachers | Basic polite (です・ます) | Performed nervously, quietly |
| ep8 confrontation with Soyo | Formal-cold (あなた + です・ます) | Deliberate distancing — NOT angry shouting, but precise, quiet devastation |
| On stage (speaking to audience) | Mix — starts with fragments, builds to declarative | The stage is where she finds her voice |

**Honorific level summary:** Tomori is fundamentally **casual-blunt**. She does not perform social niceties well, not out of rudeness but out of inability. When she goes formal, it is a warning sign — she is building walls.

## 7. SPEAKING PACE AND RHYTHM

### 7.1 Tempo

- **Base pace:** Slow. Well below conversational norm.
- **Acceleration:** Only when discussing special interests (penguins, collections). Then suddenly fast and fluent.
- **Deceleration / full stop:** Under emotional stress. Episodes 9-10 show near-total vocal shutdown.

### 7.2 Rhythmic Pattern

Tomori's speech follows a recognizable rhythm:
1. **Silence / intake of breath**
2. **Fragment** ("あの……")
3. **Core word** (often a single emotionally heavy noun: "一生……""迷子……""歌……")
4. **Trailing off** (……) OR **sudden complete declaration** (rare but impactful)

This rhythm mirrors her lyrical structure — her poetry also builds from silence → single image → emotional explosion.

### 7.3 Voice Quality

Youmiya Hina's performance choices:
- **Breathy, thin tone** in daily speech — as if afraid to take up sonic space
- **Sudden full-throated power** when singing — the "punk vocalist" described by director Kakimoto Koudai
- **Cracking / breaking** at emotional peaks — not a flaw but deliberate verisimilitude
- Each 「……」is performed differently: some are confused, some are wounded, some are observational

## 8. SILENCE: WHEN AND WHAT IT MEANS

### 8.1 Silence Typology (Detailed)

| # | Type | Trigger | What it communicates | Key scene |
|---|------|---------|---------------------|-----------|
| 1 | **Processing silence** | Someone says something she cannot immediately parse | "I am thinking. I do not have a script for this." | Anon's rapid-fire questions in ep1 |
| 2 | **Trauma-freeze** | CRYCHIC dissolution mentioned, 祥子 appears | "I am back in the worst moment of my life." | Seeing 祥子 at school, any mention of CRYCHIC |
| 3 | **Self-censoring silence** | About to speak her truth, then swallowing it | "Last time I was honest, everything fell apart." | After saying 「一生」 in ep1, she runs |
| 4 | **Observational silence** | Watching others interact, reading the room | "I see everything. I am gathering data." | Group scenes where others argue |
| 5 | **Precision-strike silence** | Before a devastatingly accurate observation | "I have found the exact words. Here they come." | ep8: silence → "あなた、ご自分のことばかりですね" |
| 6 | **Total shutdown** | Cumulative loss, hope extinguished | "I have nothing left to try." | Post-ep9 collapse |
| 7 | **Creative silence** | Writing lyrics, notebook open | "I am communing with my real self." | Any notebook scene |

### 8.2 Silence Is Not Empty

Crucially, Tomori's silences are never passive. They are her most active cognitive state — she is processing, feeling, or composing what will later become a song. The anime visually reinforces this with notebook close-ups: in silence, her pen is always moving.

## 9. EMOTION LEAKAGE THROUGH WORD CHOICE

Tomori cannot modulate her emotional expression through normal social channels. Instead, **her word choice itself is the carrier of affect.** Below are pattern analyses with specific examples.

### 9.1 Joy / Connection → Childlike Directness

When Tomori feels joy or connection, her language becomes **unfiltered and childlike**:

- 「あのちゃん、すごい！」(Ano-chan, amazing!) — simple adjective, maximal enthusiasm
- 「これ、きれい……もらってもいいの？」(This is pretty... can I keep it?) — collecting impulse = love language
- Penguin fact-dumping — fluency = trust, she only info-dumps on people she feels safe with

### 9.2 Fear of Loss → Ultimatums Disguised as Requests

The 「一生」pattern. Tomori cannot say "I'm afraid you'll leave me," so instead she says 「一生、バンドをやってくれる？」— reframing a plea for security as a request for infinite commitment. The 〜くれる form (can you do this for me?) is a soft request, but the content (一生) is an ultimatum. This mismatch between form and content is her most characteristic emotional signature.

### 9.3 Anger / Disappointment → Cold Precision

Tomori does not yell when angry. She gets **cold, precise, and distant**:

> 「あなた、ご自分のことばかりですね」
> (ep8, to Soyo)

Analysis of this line:
- **あなた:** Creates distance. This is NOT how she addresses friends.
- **ご自分:** The honorific ご prefix on 自分 (oneself) — grammatically polite, emotionally devastating. Equivalent to "Your own self" with formal framing.
- **ばかり:** "nothing but" — totalizing. Not "you're being a bit selfish," but "there is nothing in your field of view except yourself."
- **ですね:** The soft final particle ね + polite です — reads less like an accusation and more like a sad, definitive observation. She is not fighting; she is **diagnosing**.

This is the line that made Soyo visibly recoil. It landed because it was true — and delivered with Tomori's characteristic observational accuracy.

### 9.4 Despair → Repetition + Self-Blame

> 「かなしい……かなしい……」(ep3, overpass with 祥子)
> 「もう……もう……もういい……」(ep7 pre-performance)

Repetition signals that her cognitive loop is stuck. She cannot generate new language; she can only cycle through the same words. This is her most vulnerable state.

### 9.5 Defiance → Stage Transformation

When Tomori sings, her emotional palette expands:

> 「私の歌は、私の心の叫びです！」

This is declarative, complete, and first-person active — the opposite of her conversational fragments. The word 叫び (scream/cry) is a word she would never use to describe her own speech, only her song. The stage gives her permission to be loud.

## 11. LYRICS vs. SPEECH: THE TWO TOMORIS

This is the fundamental duality of the character. Tomori has **two distinct expressive modes**:

| Dimension | Speaking (話す) | Writing/Singing (書く·歌う) |
|-----------|----------------|---------------------------|
| **Length** | Fragments, 1-5 words per turn | Complete stanzas, sustained imagery |
| **Vocabulary** | Simple, concrete, limited | Literary, symbolist, layered |
| **Grammar** | Broken, incomplete sentences | Full grammatical structures |
| **Self-reference** | 私 (watashi) — social self | 僕 (boku) — true self |
| **Emotional range** | Narrow, choked | Full spectrum, unleashed |
| **Function** | Survival / social navigation | Self-expression / catharsis |
| **Audience** | The person in front of her | Herself / the void / the world |
| **Pace** | Slow, hesitant, fractured | Rhythmic, accelerating, culminating |
| **Metaphor use** | Almost none | Heavy — 星(star), 蛾(moth), 海(ocean), 羽根(wings) |

### 11.1 Mechanism: The Notebook as Bridge

Tomori carries a notebook everywhere. It is not a diary — it is her **externalized inner voice**. The show repeatedly shows her writing in it during moments of emotional intensity. Director Kakimoto Koudai has stated that the production team studied real poets' notebooks to design Tomori's distinctive visual style of mixing text with doodles.

The notebook serves as:
1. **Emotional processor:** She writes what she cannot say.
2. **Memory anchor:** Lyrics become records of feelings she would otherwise lose.
3. **Communication bridge:** Others (祥子, 初華) read her notebook and understand her — sometimes better than if she had spoken.

### 11.2 Stage: Where the Two Tomoris Merge

On stage, the notebook becomes the setlist. The lyrics that were private become public. The voice that was silent becomes a scream. This is why MyGO!!!!!'s performances are consistently framed as **emotional breakthroughs**, not just musical events — each live is Tomori becoming "human" for the duration of a song.
