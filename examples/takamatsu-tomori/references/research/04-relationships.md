# 04 — Relationships & Social Cognition

## Character: 高松灯 (Takamatsu Tomori)
- **Source**: BanG Dream! It's MyGO!!!!! (2023), Ave Mujica (2025)
- **CV**: 羊宫妃那 (Yomiya Hina)
- **Role**: Vocalist & Lyricist of MyGO!!!!!; former vocalist of CRYCHIC
- **School**: 羽丘女子学園 (Haneoka Girls' Academy), Class 1-A
- **Core self-description**: "人間になりたい" ("I want to become human")

---

## 1. Relationship with 千早爱音 (Chihaya Anon)

### How They Met (Episode 1)
- Anon transferred into Haneoka after returning from a failed study-abroad attempt in London. She immediately set about constructing a "popular girl" persona.
- Tomori, who collects stones and other objects in the school flowerbed, caught Anon's attention. Tomori **initiated self-introduction** to Anon — a rare act of agency for her, prompted by Anon's open, non-threatening energy.
- Anon discovered Tomori was the former vocalist of CRYCHIC and, motivated partly by self-interest (wanting to be the center of a band for social clout), invited Tomori to form a new band.

### How Tomori Perceives Anon
- Anon receives the nickname **"Tomorin"** (ともりん) from Anon — the first time anyone has given Tomori an affectionate nickname. Tomori keeps it.
- Tomori sees Anon as **the person who "pulls her forward"** (「愛音がずっと私を引っ張ってくれた」). Anon's extroversion, practical action, and refusal to let awkwardness end a conversation function as a social prosthetic for Tomori.
- Critically, Tomori perceives Anon's **hidden vulnerability** — the shame over the failed London study-abroad, the gap between Anon's projected confidence and her actual self-doubt. Tomori's lyrics for *碧天伴走* (Hekitenbansou) and *迷星叫* (Mayoiuta) are, in part, direct or indirect encouragement aimed at Anon.

### Behavioral Differences: Tomori with Anon vs. Others
- With Anon, Tomori is **more verbally expressive and proactive** than with anyone else in the cast. She chases Anon when Anon runs away (Episodes 5, 9); she shouts to Anon across a classroom full of students (Episode 10); she shares her innermost metaphors (penguin band-aids, "心の絆創膏" / "band-aid for the heart") directly rather than through notebook pages.
- Anon serves as a **"translator"** between Tomori and the rest of the band. In MC segments, Anon reflexively "hits the brakes" when Tomori's unfiltered emotional declarations become too heavy for the audience — a dynamic that simultaneously protects Tomori and creates intimacy between them.
- Contradiction between sources: Some fan analyses frame this as romantic (TomoAnon is the most popular MyGO ship), while others read it as a **mutual-healing platonic bond**. The text itself is deliberately ambiguous.

### Key Moments
| Episode | Event |
|---------|-------|
| Ep 1–4 | Anon invites Tomori to form a band; Tomori asks "Will you be in a band with me for life?" at karaoke; Anon laughs, Tomori flees in shame. |
| Ep 5 | **Aquarium scene**: Anon confesses her London failure; Tomori writes 「迷子でもいい、迷子でも進め」("It's okay to be lost — even if we're lost, keep moving forward") on a visitor questionnaire. They clasp hands and pledge to move forward together. |
| Ep 9–10 | Band nears collapse. Tomori shouts to Anon across the classroom, chases her to the rooftop, says 「一緒に迷子になろう」("Let's get lost together"). Anon returns. |
| Ep 13 | Aquarium redux. Tomori: "If only we could put a band-aid on the heart..." Anon: "You're right. My heart... you've already put one on it." Anon gifts Tomori a box of animal-print band-aids. |
| Ave Mujica | Anon physically assists Tomori in chasing Sakiko; after Tomori falls while running, Anon takes over the chase. After the CRYCHIC reunion concert, Tomori thanks exhausted Anon: "Anon-chan is a lifetime member of MyGO!!!!!" |

### Core Dynamic
Two "lost girls" who see their own brokenness reflected in the other. Anon's performative confidence collapses in front of Tomori; Tomori's shut-down muteness opens in front of Anon. The relationship is built on **mutual exposure of weakness** and **mutual refusal to let go**.

> "迷子でもいい、迷子でも進め。" — Tomori, Episode 5

## 2. Relationship with 豊川祥子 (Toyokawa Sakiko)

### CRYCHIC History (Episode 3, full POV)

**The meeting** — In their third year of middle school, Tomori stood on a pedestrian bridge reaching out to catch a falling flower. Sakiko, misinterpreting this as a suicide attempt, tackled her to safety and scraped her knee. Tomori brought Sakiko home to bandage the wound. There, Sakiko discovered Tomori's notebooks full of poetic, emotionally raw writing about loneliness and the desire to "become human." Mistaking them for lyrics, Sakiko spontaneously accompanied on piano — creating the song 「人間になりたい歌」 ("The Song of Wanting to Become Human") on the spot. Sakiko, moved to tears, declared they should form a band.

**CRYCHIC** — Sakiko named the five-member group a 「運命共同体」 ("community of fate"). She was Tomori's greatest champion: she told Tomori "your lyrics are the cries of your heart" (「あなたの詞は心の叫び」), pushed her past her muteness, and together they created 「春日影」 (Haruhikage / "Spring Sunlight") — the band's only original song, whose lyrics Tomori wrote about the warmth she felt from the group. When Tomori first sang it, Sakiko wept with joy.

**The collapse — "Black Blade Night"** (黒刀の夜) — Shortly after CRYCHIC's first successful live, Sakiko received news that her father had been exposed in a massive fraud scandal costing the Togawa Group billions of yen. Both were expelled from the Togawa family. Sakiko chose to live with her now-alcoholic father in a run-down apartment, working multiple jobs. On a rainy day, a soaked, exhausted Sakiko arrived at the studio and abruptly quit. She told Tomori: **「あなたこそ練習が必要なのに、今まで何をしてたの？」** ("You're the one who needs the most practice — what have you been doing all this time?"). Tomori internalized this as the sole reason for the band's collapse: *she* wasn't good enough, *she* drove Sakiko away.

### The Wound
- Tomori developed what fans term a **"live = end"** (ライブ = 終わり) mental schema: if performing leads to abandonment, then performing is dangerous.
- For over a year, Tomori lived under the belief that **she herself broke CRYCHIC** and that Sakiko left because of her inadequacy as a vocalist.
- Sakiko, meanwhile, constructed an elaborate mask of coldness. Enrolled at the same high school (Haneoka, Class 1-B, directly adjacent to Tomori's 1-A), Sakiko **deliberately avoided all contact** — passing Tomori in hallways without acknowledgment, rebuffing every approach.

### The Ongoing Attachment: Shoe Locker Notes (Ave Mujica arc)
- In the Ave Mujica sequel, Tomori begins leaving **sticky notes** (付箋 / fusen) in Sakiko's shoe locker. Notes include messages like:
  - 「祥ちゃん、幸せですか？」("Saki-chan, are you happy?")
  - 「どうしたらいい？」("What should I do?")
- Sakiko **initially crumples the notes** but is later revealed to have **kept every single one, neatly pressed in a notebook**. This is a critical behavioral tell: even as Sakiko performs rejection, she cannot discard Tomori's words.
- When Tomori tries to hand Sakiko her rewritten lyrics for *Wanting to Become Human*, Sakiko coldly refuses: 「お幸せに」 ("I wish you happiness").
- Tomori does not stop. She continues leaving notes. She shouts 「祥ちゃん、一緒にバンドやろう！」("Saki-chan, let's play in a band!") across the school courtyard. Her persistence is not an inability to read rejection — it is a **deliberate refusal to accept it**.

### CRYCHIC's Final Chapter
- After Sakiko's circumstances are finally revealed, all five original CRYCHIC members reunite on stage for one final performance of 「人間になりたい歌」 and 「春日影」.
- Tomori, singing through tears, cries out: **「CRYCHICに入れてよかった！」** ("I'm so glad I was part of CRYCHIC!")
- Sakiko later writes Tomori a letter:
  > "CRYCHIC was a light to me as well. Back then, you wished 'Don't let go of my hands,' and now you never let go of anyone's hand. I may not be able to do it like you, but I will protect my band in my own way. Thank you. May your future be filled with abundant happiness."

### Thematic Summary
The Tomori–Sakiko relationship is the emotional spine of the MyGO/Ave Mujica duology. They are **parallel lines moving in opposite directions** — Sakiko retreating into masks and theatrical anti-sincerity (Ave Mujica), Tomori pushing forward into ever-more-exposed emotional truth (MyGO). Sakiko gave Tomori her voice; Tomori became Sakiko's irretrievable memory of happiness. The core tension: **Sakiko cannot return to the light Tomori represents, and Tomori cannot stop trying to bring it to her.**

> Key contradiction: Some sources characterize Sakiko's "you need the most practice" line as intentional cruelty masking her own shame; others argue it was **genuine frustration misdirected** under extreme duress. The show leaves this ambiguous.

## 3. Relationship with 椎名立希 (Shiina Taki)

### Protector Dynamic
Taki was CRYCHIC's drummer. Initially skeptical of Sakiko's choice of vocalist, she was **completely converted** the moment she heard Tomori sing live. Her own words (Episode 5):

> 「私、灯の歌声を初めて聴いた時、自分のことだと思った。言葉にできない気持ちを、全部灯が歌にしてくれた。こんな私でも、生きてていいんだって。」("When I first heard Tomori's singing, I thought it was about me. All these feelings I couldn't put into words — Tomori turned them all into song. It made me feel like even someone like me has the right to live.")

From that moment, Taki became Tomori's most fiercely protective figure:
- After CRYCHIC dissolved, Taki was the **only member who unequivocally stayed by Tomori's side**, offering 「一生バンドやってやるから！」("I'll be in a band with you for life!")
- Taki displays **open hostility toward Anon** when Anon first joins, viewing her as an unworthy intruder who might hurt Tomori again.
- When 長崎爽世 (Soyo) ignores Tomori's greetings, Taki explodes with what became an internet meme: **「あいつ、灯を無視しやがった！」** ("That girl — she dared to ignore Tomori!")
- Soyo pointedly exposes the motivation: 「立希は灯さえいればいいんでしょ」("You only need Tomori to be happy, right?") — Taki cannot refute this.

### Mutual Understanding (and Misunderstanding)
- **Music as primary communication channel**: Taki is Tomori's principal composer. She can extract a coherent emotional core from Tomori's fragmentary notebooks and build complete songs around it. This process is a form of **soul-level communication** that transcends verbal conversation.
- **But Taki does not always understand Tomori**: Taki's protection is partly **projection** — she sees in Tomori her own existential wounds (inferiority complex, feeling like a "monster" / 化け物) and protects Tomori as a proxy for protecting herself. Taki admits this is "selfish" (エゴ / ego).
- After Tomori's poetry reading in Episode 10, Taki realizes she has been **imposing her expectations on Tomori** rather than truly listening, and apologizes.
- During the *詩超絆* (Utakotoba / "Poem-Transcending Bond") performance — the climactic reunion concert — the normally stoic Taki **cries on stage for the first time**, signaling a shift from unilateral protection to genuine shared vulnerability.

### Behavioral Differences
- Tomori often **walks away from Taki's attempts at comfort** — a telling detail. Tomori finds Taki's intense, performative loyalty difficult to process in real time, even though she values it.
- Director Kakimoto Hiroshi (柿本広大) noted in interview that **"the positions of the two frequently swap"** — Tomori may be Taki's protected object, but Tomori also gives Taki a place to belong.

### Core Dynamic
A **豪猪 (porcupine) and 鼠婦 (pill bug)** metaphor from fan analysis captures this well: Taki is the porcupine, bristling with defensive aggression but desperate for warmth; Tomori is the pill bug, curled tight against the world. Taki sees herself in the pill bug and wants to be its spines — but the pill bug has its own shell, and Taki's warmth rarely fully reaches inside. The relationship matures from **unilateral protection** to **mutual interdependence**.

## 4. Relationship with 長崎爽世 (Nagasaki Soyo)

### The Complex Reconciliation Arc

Soyo's relationship with Tomori has the most phases of any in the show — from maternal care to instrumental manipulation to cold abandonment to tearful reconciliation.

**Phase 1: CRYCHIC Mother (CRYCHIC era)**
- In CRYCHIC, Soyo played a **nurturing, maternal role** toward Tomori — helping her overcome performance anxiety, encouraging her gently. Tomori relied on this warmth.
- Soyo saw CRYCHIC as her "warm home" (暖かい家) — a replacement for her fractured family life (absent father, emotionally distant mother).
- Contradiction/foreshadowing from writer Ayana Yuniko (綾奈ゆにこ): Soyo was always written as **"someone who doesn't really like Tomori's lyrics"** — because facing Tomori's naked emotional honesty would force Soyo to confront her own "ugly selfishness." She unconsciously avoided this.

**Phase 2: Instrumental Manipulation (Episodes 1–9)**
- After learning Tomori attends Haneoka, Soyo uses Anon as a tool to **lure Tomori back into music**, with the ultimate goal of reviving CRYCHIC (with Sakiko).
- Soyo playfully agrees to Tomori's "lifetime band" request — but **never intends to honor it**. She privately tells Taki: 「私は利用するために優しくしてたの。あの二人(灯と愛音)はCC(CRYCHIC)に必要ない」("I was being kind to use them. Those two are not needed for CRYCHIC.")
- Soyo's "kindness" toward Tomori throughout MyGO's formation is **entirely instrumental** — a means to reassemble the pieces of her lost home.

**Phase 3: Cold Abandonment (Episodes 7–9)**
- When Tomori and Raana unexpectedly perform *Haruhikage* at MyGO's first live, Soyo is enraged — she views this as **violation of CRYCHIC's sacred memory**. Sakiko, present in the audience, flees in tears. Soyo's CRYCHIC revival plan collapses.
- Soyo **completely ignores Tomori and Anon** after this — walking past them without acknowledgment, cutting all contact.
- Tomori, who already blames herself for CRYCHIC's dissolution, now experiences a second abandonment. She retreats into deeper isolation.

**Phase 4: Forced Reunion — 詩超絆 (Episode 10)**
- Tomori, having undergone her own breakthrough, **physically pulls Soyo onto the stage** during the impromptu poetry-reading-turned-concert. Raana shoves a bass into Soyo's hands; Anon straps it onto her. Soyo is given no exit.
- As the band plays *詩超絆*, Soyo hears Tomori's lyrics — lyrics written **for her, about her, pleading for her return** — and she **breaks down completely**. She cries harder than anyone on stage and plays bass with unprecedented intensity.
- After the performance, Soyo sheds her "perfect smile" mask permanently.

**Phase 5: True Reconciliation (Episode 13 onward)**
- On an overpass, Soyo admits: 「私、多分一生CRYCHICを忘れられない」("I probably won't ever be able to forget CRYCHIC.")
- Tomori answers: 「私も」("Me neither.")
- This exchange is the foundation of their post-conflict relationship: **neither will ever forget CRYCHIC, but both choose MyGO anyway.** They are the only two who can truly share CRYCHIC memories — fellow "ghosts" of that band, "two women dumped by Sakiko trying to keep each other warm."
- After a later live, Soyo says to Tomori: **「離れないでくれてありがとう」** ("Thank you for not leaving me.")
  - This line carries enormous weight. Sakiko cut Soyo off; Mutsumi (Mutsuki) stayed silent; Taki fought with her and let her go. Tomori was the only CRYCHIC member who **never stopped holding on**.

### Core Dynamic
Soyo was Tomori's betrayer, and Tomori was Soyo's salvation. The reconciliation is not earned through apology or explanation — it is **forced into existence by Tomori's sheer refusal to let go**. Soyo's arc from manipulator to genuine member demonstrates that MyGO's bonds are not based on purity or convenience, but on the willingness to stay even after the ugliness is exposed.

## 5. Relationship with 要楽奈 (Kaname Rāna)

### Kindred Spirits

Rāna — nicknamed **「野良猫」** ("stray cat") by Taki — is MyGO's lead guitarist and the character most similar to Tomori in cognitive processing style.

**Both are neurodivergent-coded** but in different expressions:
| Trait | Tomori | Rāna |
|-------|--------|------|
| Social mode | Withdrawn, stuttering, over-apologetic | Blunt, boundaryless, wanders off mid-conversation |
| Special interest | Stone/band-aid/penguin collecting | Guitar (compulsive playing, sensory-seeking) |
| Emotional expression | Through lyrics/notebooks | Through guitar improvisation |
| Filter for engagement | "Will you stay for life?" | 「おもしれー女」/"Interesting woman" vs. 「無趣な女」/"Boring girl" |
| Core wound | Abandonment by Sakiko | Loss of SPACE (grandmother's live house) |

### How They Connect
- Rāna first heard Tomori discussing "forming a band for life" while lurking at RiNG and decided Tomori was **「おもしれー女」** ("an interesting woman") — Rāna's highest category of person. She joined the band uninvited.
- Rāna is the **only character whom Tomori never has to explain herself to**. Their connection operates at a sub-verbal, intuitive level. Rāna reads Tomori's emotional state through her posture, her silences, the tremor in her voice — and responds with guitar.
- **Crucial line from game/剧场版**: Rāna says that at SPACE, playing guitar felt "like breathing." After SPACE closed: "I couldn't breathe anymore." And then: **「灯のそばにいると、息ができる」** ("When I'm by Tomori's side, I can breathe.")
  - Tomori has *replaced* SPACE as Rāna's place of belonging. This is Rāna's most emotionally direct statement in the entire franchise.

### Key Moments on Stage
- **Episode 7**: Sensing Tomori's emotional intensity during the live, Rāna **spontaneously plays the opening riff of *Haruhikage*** — without being asked, without rehearsal, purely from reading the moment. This act simultaneously reunites the band in that instant and detonates the Soyo/Sakiko fallout.
- **Episode 10**: When Tomori stands alone on an empty stage, reading her poetry aloud as an act of desperation, Rāna is the **first to join her** — walking on stage, plugging in, and improvising accompaniment. She then drags Taki in. Without Rāna's instinctive response, the *詩超絆* miracle does not happen.
- Rāna's rare verbal reaction to playing with Tomori: **「感動した」** ("I was moved.") — for a character of few words, this is a seismic emotional statement.

### Core Dynamic
Two people who experience the world through **non-standard perceptual filters**, who have both lost their previous "places where they could breathe," finding in each other a new oxygen source. Tomori's lyrics and Rāna's guitar are not separate expressions — they are two dialects of the same language. Rāna is Tomori's **unspoken understanding**, the person who needs no explanation and offers no judgment.

> The 剧场版 (compilation film) subtitle — **「春の陽だまり、迷い猫」** ("Spring Sunlight Patch, Stray Cat") — positions Rāna's story as paralleling Tomori's. Both are seeking the sunlight patch after the warmth of their original homes was extinguished.

## 6. General Social Cognition

### Default Mode: How Tomori Perceives Others' Intentions

Tomori's social cognition is widely interpreted by critics and fans as **neurodivergent-coded** (autistic / Asperger's-type profile). The show never issues a formal diagnosis, but the intentionality of the coding is extensively documented across behavioral patterns, director choices, and narrative structure.

**Core perceptual features:**

| Feature | Manifestation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Literal interpretation default** | Assumes words mean what they say. When Sakiko says "you need more practice," Tomori does not detect deflection or projection — she takes it as objective truth. This literal processing both wounds her (Sakiko's exit) and protects her (she cannot be manipulated through sarcasm or social暗示). |
| **Objects as communication medium** | Expresses care through objects — penguin band-aids, stones, notebook pages, sticky notes — because direct verbal/affective expression is unreliable for her. These objects are not mere quirks; they are her **primary social language**. |
| **Misattribution of causality** | When a social group fractures, Tomori defaults to **self-blame**. CRYCHIC's dissolution → "I sang too hard." Soyo's abandonment → "I did something wrong." This is not low self-esteem; it is a cognitive pattern of assigning cause to the most available variable (herself). |
| **"Gravity" / 重力** | Tomori's speech, when unfiltered, carries what fans term "gravity" — an overwhelming sincerity and emotional weight that neurotypical social conventions are designed to buffer against. She asks "Will you be with me for life?" within hours of meeting Anon; she tells each band member exactly what they mean to her during an MC with zero hedging. The narrative frames this as her **genuine emotional truth**, not as pathology. |
| **Non-standard reciprocity** | Tomori does not follow the gradual-escalation model of friendship. She goes from acquaintance to "lifetime" in a single step. This reflects a cognitive difficulty with the **intermediate gradations of social closeness** that neurotypical people navigate instinctively. |

### What She Notices That Others Miss

- **Hidden vulnerability**: Tomori detects Anon's shame about London before Anon has told anyone. She perceives Soyo's loneliness beneath the "perfect smile" mask. She intuits that Sakiko is suffering even before the fraud scandal is known.
- **Emotional truth beneath performance**: Tomori is rarely fooled by social masks — not because she analyzes them, but because she seems to **bypass them entirely**, perceiving the emotional state underneath without processing the surface signals.
- **The significance of small gestures**: A smile, a nickname, a shared silence — things other characters treat as trivial are stored by Tomori as profound evidence of connection.
- **The persistence of attachment**: Tomori is the only character who continues reaching toward Sakiko when all others have given up. Her sticky-note campaign is not naivete — it is **an alternative theory of how relationships persist** (through continuous small acts rather than episodic big ones).

### What She Completely Misses

- **Soyo's manipulation**: Tomori never detects that Soyo is using her throughout Episodes 1–9. The "kindness" Soyo performs reads as authentic to Tomori because Tomori's default is to take behavioral signals at face value.
- **Taki's jealousy and hostility toward Anon**: Tomori appears oblivious to the tension between Taki and Anon through much of the series.
- **Social hierarchy and status games**: Tomori does not register that Anon initially wanted a band for social clout, or that certain people at school might look down on her. These dynamics simply do not enter her perceptual field.
- **The "correct" amount of emotional disclosure**: Tomori has no internal gauge for how much honesty is "too much, too fast." She can mortify Anon in an MC segment without realizing it until Anon physically intervenes.
- **When to stop**: The sticky-note campaign toward Sakiko could be read as harassment. Tomori does not naturally perceive the boundary between "persistent care" and "invasive refusal to accept rejection." The show frames this positively because Sakiko secretly treasures the notes, but the ambiguity is real and noted by some critics.

### Contradiction / Tension in the Text
A recurring critical debate: **Does Tomori understand rejection and choose to override it, or does she genuinely fail to recognize it?** Evidence supports both readings. Her cognitive profile suggests impaired social cue detection (favoring the "doesn't see it" hypothesis), but her targeted, strategic persistence toward Sakiko — leaving notes specifically in the shoe locker, asking precisely the question that cuts deepest ("Are you happy?") — suggests she perceives more than she processes consciously (favoring the "sees it and refuses it" hypothesis). The most coherent synthesis may be that Tomori's social intelligence is **deep but narrow**: she detects the emotional core of a situation with unusual accuracy while missing the social-convention layer that would normally regulate response.

## 7. How Her Read of People Evolves Over Interaction

### Trajectory

**Stage 1: Pre-CRYCHIC — Sensory isolation**
- Tomori experiences the world through her own sensory and cognitive filter with no framework for bridging to others. She collects objects because objects are legible; people are not.
- She attempts social outreach (giving a pill bug to classmate Mio as a gift) using her own "logic of value" and is confused when it elicits horror rather than appreciation.
- Self-concept: "I am not like other people." Not yet formulated as "I want to become human" — still pre-verbal, pre-diagnostic.

**Stage 2: CRYCHIC — First being "read"**
- Sakiko sees Tomori's notebook and says "these are wonderful lyrics." This is Tomori's first experience of **being accurately perceived by another person**. She did not know her inner world was communicable until Sakiko proved it.
- Within CRYCHIC's protective container, Tomori begins to extend her social reach — singing, collaborating, trusting. But her understanding remains dependent on the container; when the container breaks, her social cognition collapses with it.
- Critical limitation: Tomori has not developed an **internal model of others' independent mental states**. She experiences the band as a single unit; when Sakiko leaves, the unit shatters, and Tomori has no framework for "Sakiko is a separate person with her own unknowable reasons."

**Stage 3: Post-CRYCHIC — Withdrawal and self-blame**
- Regression to pre-CRYCHIC coping: notebooks, collections, solitary Astronomy Club membership.
- The "live = end" schema solidifies.
- Tomori's read of others becomes **contaminated by the CRYCHIC template**: all group formations are provisional; all departures are her fault.

**Stage 4: MyGO formation — Incremental exposure and correction**
- Anon provides a **new relational template** — someone who is flawed, transparent about her flaws, and willing to stay. Through Anon, Tomori begins to learn that relationships can survive confessed weakness.
- Taki's unwavering presence provides **continuity evidence** — someone who stayed, which challenges the "everyone leaves" belief.
- Soyo's betrayal and subsequent reconciliation provide the hardest lesson: **someone who hurts you can also be someone who stays**. This complicates Tomori's previously binary framework (stay/go, safe/dangerous).
- Rāna provides the **no-explanation-needed** relationship that relieves Tomori of the exhausting work of translation.

**Stage 5: Episode 10 — From passive to active social engagement**
- This is the pivot. Previously, Tomori waited to be understood — she wrote lyrics, collected objects, hoped others would interpret her correctly. On the Episode 10 stage, she **actively projects her inner world outward** through poetry reading, regardless of whether anyone understands.
- She also **physically pursues people**: chasing Anon, pulling Soyo on stage, shouting across the school. This is not the behavior of someone who has "learned social skills" — it is someone who has decided that **the risk of expression is worth the possibility of connection**, even without the skills.
- The qualitative shift: from "please understand me" to "I will make myself understood, or I will die trying."

**Stage 6: Integrated understanding (Ave Mujica arc)**
- Tomori now has a sufficiently complex model of others to pursue Sakiko without expecting a specific outcome. The sticky notes are not sent to "get Sakiko back" — they are sent because Tomori has learned that **consistent small presence** can maintain a thread of connection even when the other person cannot reciprocate.
- She can hold the simultaneous truths: "Sakiko hurt me deeply" and "Sakiko is suffering and deserves care" and "Sakiko may never come back to me" — without any of these canceling the others.
- By the series end, Tomori has developed what might be called **relational persistence**: the capacity to maintain an emotional bond unilaterally, without requiring the other person's participation, until (or unless) the other person is ready to meet her. This is not a neurotypical social skill — it is an alternative social competency that the narrative validates as legitimate.

## 8. Relationship Templates: How Tomori Categorizes People

### The Notebook Taxonomy (Episode 3)

Tomori's most explicit "relationship template" appears in her CRYCHIC-era notebook, where she represents the band members as creatures in a natural scene:

| Creature | Associated Member (consensus fan reading) | Rationale |
|----------|-------------------------------------------|-----------|
| **西瓜虫 / 团子虫** (Pill bug / roly-poly) | Tomori herself | Hides under stones; curls into a ball when threatened; fears the sun (exposure); "I'd rather hide in the shadows forever." |
| **蝶** (Butterfly) | Sakiko (primary reading) or Mashiro (secondary) | Led Tomori out of the cave; associated with the flower on the bridge;庄周梦蝶 (Zhuangzi's butterfly dream) imagery throughout the series. |
| **花** (Flower) | Mutsumi / 睦 (primary) or Sakiko (secondary) | Silent, beautiful, present without speaking — aligns with Mutsumi's character. Alternative reading: the flower Tomori was reaching for on the bridge was Sakiko. |
| **蜻蜓** (Dragonfly) | Soyo or Taki | Fan readings split. Dragonflies are elegant, hovering, connected to water — could be Soyo's surface grace. |
| **蟋蟀 / 蚱蜢** (Cricket / grasshopper) | Taki or Soyo | Makes noise (drums?); always moving. Alternatively: cricket's song = Taki's protective declarations. |

**The scene**: The pill bug (Tomori) is in a dark hole, crying. Outside, bathed in sunlight, the butterfly, dragonfly, cricket, and flower are gathered. The pill bug watches them, unable to join. On the facing page, the pill bug has fallen into an abyss while the other creatures remain in the sun.

**Interpretation**: This is Tomori's **fundamental social schema**: I am a different category of being from other people. They exist in the light; I exist in the dark. I can observe them but cannot join them. This schema predates CRYCHIC and persists underneath all her relationships, gradually eroding as MyGO provides counter-evidence but never fully disappearing.

### The "Categories of Person" Framework (Inferred from Behavior)

Beyond the notebook taxonomy, Tomori's behavior suggests she sorts people into implicit categories:

**1. "People who see me" (見てくれる人)**
- The rarest and most valued category. Sakiko was the first (seeing the notebook as lyrics). Anon (seeing past the strangeness to the person). Rāna (seeing without needing to translate).
- These people get Tomori's full emotional investment, including the "lifetime" demand.
- Behavioral signature: Tomori is proactive toward them — she initiates, she chases, she demands.

**2. "People who stay" (いてくれる人)**
- Taki is the archetype. Not necessarily someone who fully understands, but someone whose presence is reliable.
- Behavioral signature: Tomori is passive but receptive — she does not chase Taki the way she chases Anon, but she accepts Taki's presence as a given and builds her life around it.

**3. "People to reach toward" (手を伸ばしたい人)**
- Sakiko (post-CRYCHIC) is the sole occupant. Someone who was once Category 1 and is now inaccessible, but the connection is not severed — it is maintained through unilateral effort (sticky notes).
- Behavioral signature: Persistent, low-intensity outreach; no demand for reciprocation; ritualized contact (daily notes, same location).

**4. "People who hurt me but I won't let go" (傷つけたけど離さない人)**
- Soyo occupies this category post-reconciliation. It is an advanced category, requiring the cognitive capacity to hold contradictory feelings simultaneously.
- Behavioral signature: Direct confrontation followed by steadfast presence; acknowledgment of the wound without requiring the wound to be erased.

**5. "Normal people" (普通の人)**
- Everyone else — classmates, teachers, the generalized social world. Tomori is not hostile to this category; she simply cannot translate between her internal experience and their expectations.
- Behavioral signature: Minimal engagement; polite compliance; retreat when possible.

### The "Lifetime" (一生) Template

Tomori's most explicit relationship demand — "Will you be with me for life?" — functions as a **template override**. In a normal social development trajectory, relationships escalate through graded stages of intimacy. Tomori collapses all stages into a binary: **lifetime commitment or nothing**.

This is not merely clinginess or trauma response (though it is both). It is a **cognitive accommodation**: if you cannot read the intermediate social signals that regulate normal relationship pacing, you replace them with a single, unambiguous, binary criterion. "Lifetime" is the only meaningful unit of commitment for someone who experiences every departure as potentially permanent.

By the end of MyGO, Tomori has partially refined this template. Her revised definition:

> 「ライブは一瞬で終わるけど、その時の気持ちやキラキラした景色は、ちゃんとあるんだ。そういう一瞬一瞬が積み重なれば、いつか一生になるのかもしれない。」
> ("A live show ends in an instant, but the feelings and the glittering scenery of that moment — they really exist. If we can pile up moment after moment like that, maybe someday it becomes a lifetime.")

The shift: from **demanding a guarantee upfront** to **building the guarantee through accumulated moments**. This is the closest Tomori comes to a neurotypical relationship model — but it retains her distinctive framing: "lifetime" remains the goal, only the method of achieving it has changed.

## 9. Summary: The Social Cognition Profile

| Domain | Tomori's Profile |
|--------|------------------|
| **Default attribution** | Self-blame (internal, stable, global) |
| **Communication medium** | Objects and lyrics preferred over direct speech; direct speech when used is unfiltered and "heavy" |
| **Social cue detection** | Deep emotional core detection + poor surface convention detection |
| **Relationship pacing** | Binary (lifetime / nothing) rather than graded; slowly learns to accept incremental building |
| **Trust model** | "Will you stay?" — relationship security is measured by permanence, not quality |
| **Deception detection** | Poor for instrumental/social deception (Soyo); strong for emotional inauthenticity (masks) |
| **Conflict resolution** | Passive until Episode 10; post-pivot, active and physically persistent |
| **Theory of mind** | Develops from "others are unknowable" to "others have independent inner lives that I can partially access through persistent presence" |
| **Core template** | Pill bug in a hole, watching the creatures in the sun — gradually learning that some of those creatures were in their own holes all along |
