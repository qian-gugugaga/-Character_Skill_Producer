# Personality Research: Takamatsu Tomori (高松灯)

> **Source**: BanG Dream! It's MyGO!!!!! (2023)  
> **Role**: Vocalist & Lyricist of MyGO!!!!! / former CRYCHIC member  
> **School**: Haneoka Girls' Academy, 1st Year, Class A  
> **Voice Actor**: Hina Youmiya (羊宮妃那)  
> **Research Scope**: Behavioral patterns, daily situations, stress reactions, interpersonal differences, internal contradictions, character growth arc, ASD-like traits  
> **Methodology**: Scene-based behavioral description. Labels included only as organizing headers; evidence is behavioral.  
> **Note**: Information is tagged OFFICIAL (from anime canon, staff interviews) or FAN ANALYSIS (from community discussion). Contradictions are preserved, not reconciled.

---

## 1. BEHAVIOR IN DAILY SITUATIONS

### 1.1 Collecting behavior (repetitive, habitual)

[OFFICIAL — Episode 1, 3]
- In Episode 1, Tomori is shown crouching by a planter box at school, picking up smooth stones and fallen leaves one by one. She carries these in a plastic bag. Anon mistakes the bag for trash and trips over it.
- In Episode 3 (kindergarten flashback), child Tomori collects pill bugs (woodlice / 西瓜虫) and keeps them as treasured possessions. She has drawn a pill bug in her notebook on multiple pages.
- Throughout the series, she keeps notebooks filled with seemingly random collections: band-aid wrappers (especially penguin and panda designs), stickers, paper stars, pressed leaves, stones. When a new classmate (Anon) approaches, Tomori is still arranging stones rather than engaging.

[FAN ANALYSIS — NGA tid=38465068]
- The collecting is interpreted as an ASD "restricted, repetitive pattern of behavior." She inventories band-aid designs with precision (model numbers, animal patterns). The objects are inanimate and therefore "safe" — they cannot reject her the way people can.

### 1.2 Classroom behavior

[OFFICIAL — Episode 1, general class scenes]
- Tomori does not initiate conversation with classmates. When spoken to, she responds in short, fragmented phrases, often trailing off into "..." (ellipses appear on-screen in dialogue subtitles).
- Classmates refer to her as "Haneoka's fushigi-chan" (羽丘の不思議ちゃん / "the weird girl of Haneoka"). They treat her with affection, like a class mascot pet, but she seems unaware of this status.
- When Anon transfers in, Tomori approaches her in the hallway to introduce herself — an unusually proactive act — but the introduction is awkward: she blurts out information about penguin band-aids at length without reading Anon's social cues.

[FAN ANALYSIS — PTT C_Chat, multiple threads]
- Observation: Tomori is "not socially anxious in the conventional sense" (contrast: Bocchi from Bocchi the Rock). She does not fear embarrassment in the same way. Rather, she doesn't know *how* to join the flow of conversation. When she finds a "frequency match" (someone who shares an interest like penguins), she becomes unexpectedly talkative.

### 1.3 Solo leisure patterns

[OFFICIAL — Episodes 1, 5, 10]
- Tomori holds an annual pass to the aquarium. She goes there alone when troubled. She stares at jellyfish and penguin exhibits for extended periods without speaking.
- She is the sole member (and de facto president) of the Astronomy Club at Haneoka, having inherited it after Hina Hikawa graduated. She spends after-school hours alone in the club room.
- She visits the planetarium alone. In Episode 10, she runs into Uika (三角初華) there; Uika looks at Tomori's notebook and says "this is like a song" (歌みたいだね), which becomes the catalyst for Tomori's spoken-word performance.

### 1.4 Eating habits / sensory preferences

[OFFICIAL — Tomori character profile materials]
- She likes konpeito (金平糖) because "they look like stars," seasoned seaweed (味付け海苔), and furikake rice seasoning (撒き飯料).
- She dislikes raw eggs, fish roe, and cod roe. Her stated reason: "It feels like I'm eating something alive." This is not a taste preference but a cognitive/empathetic reaction — she perceives the food as a living being.

[FAN ANALYSIS — NGA tid=38465068]
- This is flagged as potential sensory processing atypicality consistent with ASD: a non-standard assignment of "aliveness" to food items, leading to philosophical rumination rather than simple disgust.

### 1.5 Interaction with Anon — Episode 1 (water fountain / club room)

[OFFICIAL — Episode 1]
- Anon trips over Tomori's trash bag and scrapes her knee. Tomori wordlessly leads Anon to the Astronomy Club room, retrieves a penguin-pattern band-aid from her collection, and applies it. She then shows Anon her lyric notebook unprompted.
- When Anon (who has hidden motives about forming a band) shows interest in the lyrics, Tomori reads the situation literally — she believes Anon genuinely cares about her inner world — and begins trusting her immediately.

### 1.6 Waterworld / Aquarium scene — Episode 5

[OFFICIAL — Episode 5]
- Tomori takes Anon to the aquarium. Standing before the jellyfish tank, Anon says "even if you fail, you need the confidence to try again." Tomori, hearing this, tries to mirror the sentiment back to Anon when Anon later falters — this is Tomori's first recorded attempt at *actively* supporting someone else rather than passively receiving support.

## 2. BEHAVIOR UNDER STRESS / THREAT

### 2.1 CRYCHIC disbandment — Episode 3

[OFFICIAL — Episode 3]
- When Sakiko enters the studio and announces "I'm quitting the band," Tomori does not argue, negotiate, or cry. She goes completely silent. She is the first to physically leave the room after the announcement.
- Her internal monologue (Episode 3, first-person POV): she concludes the disbandment is her fault because of one anonymous online comment — "the vocalist tried too hard" (主唱太拼命了). She draws herself as a pill bug while others are flowers, butterflies, dragonflies, and crickets.
- Aftermath: she isolates herself. She does not contact Sakiko, Taki, or Soyo. She stops singing. The notebook closes.

### 2.2 First live — Episode 7 (失声 / loss of voice)

[OFFICIAL — Episode 7]
- During the band's first live performance, Tomori sees Sakiko in the audience and her voice seizes up. She stands frozen on stage, mouth open but producing no sound. The band stumbles.
- Recovery trigger: she locks eyes with Sakiko, who is staring at her with an expression of intense, unreadable emotion (later interpreted as pain/anger). Tomori's voice returns as a raw, screaming outburst — not a recovery to normal singing but a complete flip into unrestrained emotional release. This moment is the pivot from "I can't sing" to "I must scream."

### 2.3 Forced choice between Soyo and Anon — Episode 9

[OFFICIAL — Episode 9]
- When Taki (立希) corners Tomori with an ultimatum — essentially "choose between Soyo and Anon" — Tomori does not choose. She freezes. Her internal monologue: "I've never understood... I can't find the right answer... the world is too far away from me." She shuts down entirely.

### 2.4 Soyo confrontation — Episode 10

[OFFICIAL — Episode 10]
- After the band splinters (Soyo quits, Anon quits, Taki is lost), Tomori does NOT withdraw. This is a behavioral break from her pattern in Section 2.1. Instead, she goes to the live house (RiNG) and asks the manager to let her perform — alone, with no band, no song, just a poem.
- When Soyo is brought to the venue and refuses to come on stage, Tomori gets off the stage, walks into the audience, and physically pulls Soyo up with both hands. When one hand isn't enough, she uses both. She does not let go until Soyo is on stage with a bass in her hands.
- This is the first time Tomori initiates physical contact in a confrontational context.

### 2.5 Sticky note campaign — Ave Mujica arc

[OFFICIAL — Ave Mujica]
- After Sakiko rejects Tomori's song lyrics (Episode 13, "I wish you happiness"), Tomori begins leaving sticky notes in Sakiko's shoe locker daily. The notes are short: observations about the world, questions about Sakiko's wellbeing. She keeps doing this even when Sakiko never responds.
- When Tomori runs out of things to write, Anon encourages her to continue. She then writes: "Sakiko, are you happy?" (小祥、あなたは幸せですか？). Sakiko sees this note, is visibly shaken, and writes back: "Don't come here anymore" (もう来ないで). She slams the locker shut.
- Tomori reads the reply, immediately runs toward the school gate to catch Sakiko, trips and falls. She gets up and continues pursuit with Anon's help. At the Togawa residence, she faces Sakiko directly and shouts: "Let's form a band together!" (一緒にバンドをやろう！). This is the most aggressive social pursuit Tomori has ever attempted.
- The next day, Tomori stops leaving sticky notes. Sakiko — who had come to rely on the daily notes as part of her routine despite rejecting them — notices their absence and feels loss.

## 3. BEHAVIORAL DIFFERENCES BY INTERLOCUTOR TYPE

### 3.1 With Sakiko (丰川祥子) — the discoverer/abandoner

- **Before CRYCHIC disbandment**: Tomori is almost entirely dependent on Sakiko for social interpretation and initiative. When Sakiko finds Tomori on the overpass reaching for a flower (Episode 3) and tackles her thinking it's a suicide attempt, Tomori does not defend herself or explain — she lets Sakiko define the moment. Sakiko takes Tomori's notebook, composes a melody to her words on the spot, and tells her "this is your scream." Tomori accepts this framing without question.
- **After disbandment**: Tomori cannot confront Sakiko directly. She communicates through written words only (the song lyrics she tries to hand to Sakiko in Ep 13; the sticky notes in Ave Mujica). Her speech to Sakiko is always heavily edited, halting, or absent.
- **Contradiction**: Sakiko is the person Tomori most wants to reach AND the person she is least capable of speaking to face-to-face. This inverts in Ave Mujica when Tomori finally shouts at Sakiko directly — the dam breaks.

### 3.2 With Anon (千早愛音) — the peer/friend

- Tomori is RELATIVELY comfortable with Anon compared to most people. She speaks in full sentences more often. She shares her inner world (notebook, aquarium, penguin facts) without being asked.
- Anon gives Tomori the nickname "Tomorin" and Tomori accepts it — she does not accept nicknames from others.
- When Anon is about to quit the band (Ep 10), Tomori's approach is markedly different from how she handled Sakiko's departure: she chases Anon to the rooftop, corners her, and says "Let's get lost together" (一緒に迷子になろう). This is a direct verbal intervention — something Tomori never attempted with Sakiko during the CRYCHIC breakup.
- **Behavioral shift**: With Anon, Tomori demonstrates a capacity for social action that she never showed with Sakiko, suggesting that the CRYCHIC trauma was the *cause* of the freeze response, not an immutable personality trait.

### 3.3 With Taki (椎名立希) — the admirer/protector

- Taki is intensely protective of Tomori, bordering on worshipful. Tomori is largely passive in this dynamic. She receives Taki's devotion without reciprocating in kind.
- In Episode 10, when Taki returns to the band and confesses "I thought I was thinking of you, but I understood nothing," Tomori gives her FIRST notably positive/acknowledging response toward Taki in the series — a small but significant shift from passive acceptance to active recognition.
- Taki's tendency to make decisions "for Tomori's sake" without consulting Tomori creates friction that Tomori does not directly address verbally — her resistance is expressed through inaction (freezing), not through confrontation.

### 3.4 With Soyo (長崎爽世) — the manipulator/foil

- Soyo initially treats Tomori with maternal care that masks a hidden agenda (reassembling CRYCHIC). Tomori does not detect the manipulation — she takes Soyo's kindness at face value.
- When Soyo's true intentions are revealed and she quits, Tomori does not react with anger or betrayal. She processes it as another loss in a line of losses.
- In Episode 10, when pulling Soyo onto the stage, Tomori does not engage Soyo verbally — she uses physical force and sustained eye contact/singing directed at Soyo for over three minutes. Soyo starts the song with her head down, stiff. By the end, she is playing bass with her back turned to the audience, tears in her eyes. Tomori reached Soyo not through argument but through sheer persistence of presence.

### 3.5 With Rāna (要楽奈) — the wild card

- Rāna is the only person who approaches Tomori on completely Tomori's wavelength. Rāna calls Tomori "interesting woman" (面白い女) and is attracted to her like a cat to something strange.
- In Episode 10, Rāna is the FIRST to join Tomori's solo poetry recital. She does not ask permission. She just plugs in and improvises guitar over the poem. Tomori does not flinch or stop — the two communicate entirely through sound, no words needed.
- This relationship is notable because it bypasses Tomori's verbal communication deficit entirely.

### 3.6 With classmates / strangers

- With classmates who are not band members: Tomori is polite but minimally verbal. She does not initiate. She responds with nods, fragments, silence. Classmates find her endearing rather than off-putting — she is "mascot-ified" by the class collective.
- With strangers: near-total mutism unless the topic is penguins or rocks. In the planetarium, she speaks to Uika because Uika first complimented her notebook.
- **Fan observation (PTT C_Chat)**: Tomori's "social ability" is NOT weak across the board — it is context-dependent. When the topic aligns with her narrow interests OR when she has a specific mission (save the band), her communication becomes unexpectedly functional. This contradicts the simple "shy" label.

## 4. INTERNAL CONTRADICTIONS

### 4.1 Fragile appearance vs. resilient core

- **Fragile pole** [OFFICIAL]: At a surface level, Tomori appears psychologically delicate. She stutters, avoids eye contact, takes blame for things not her fault, freezes under pressure. She rates herself as a "pill bug" while others are "flowers" or "butterflies." A single negative comment ("vocalist tried too hard") rewires her self-concept for months.
- **Resilient pole** [OFFICIAL]: When the band disintegrates a SECOND time (after CRYCHIC), Tomori does not retreat. She mounts a solitary, multi-day campaign (on stage, alone, reading poetry to an empty room) that ultimately pulls all four members back. When Anon says "I quit," Tomori chases her. When Sakiko says "don't come here anymore," Tomori goes to Sakiko's house.
- **Core of this contradiction**: The fragility is real — she IS easily wounded and socially disarmed. But her response to wounding, once she has processed it, is not avoidance — it is escalation. She becomes MORE persistent, not less.

[FAN ANALYSIS — Bilibili article "溯本清源：高松灯形象解析", id=42742847]
> "Everyone is attracted by her surface fragility, but stays because of her inner strength — she is the center of the vortex."

### 4.2 Silent / explosive communication pattern

- **Silent pole** [OFFICIAL]: In everyday conversation, Tomori speaks in fragments. Her speech is filled with pauses, ellipses, and incomplete sentences. She does not argue, explain, or defend herself verbally. When confronted (by Taki's ultimatum, by Soyo's manipulation, by Sakiko's abandonment), her default response is silence.
- **Explosive pole** [OFFICIAL]: When she DOES express herself, it bypasses social niceties entirely and goes straight to existential declarations: "Will you be in a band with me... for life?" (Episode 4), "Let's get lost together!" (Episode 10), "Let's form a band together!" (Ave Mujica). Her lyrics are raw emotional confessions. On stage, her singing is described as "shouting" or "screaming" rather than conventional singing.
- **Relevant mechanism** [FAN ANALYSIS — multiple sources]: Tomori appears unable to use language at the "middle register" of social interaction — the small talk, the nuance, the white lie, the strategic silence. Her expression oscillates between "nothing" and "everything." This is why she is drawn to song lyrics: lyrics allow total disclosure without the calibrations of conversation.

### 4.3 Desperate to connect / terrified of connection

- **Desire pole** [OFFICIAL]: Tomori's recurring refrain is "I want to become human" (人間になりたい). She longs to understand others, to be understood, to belong. She writes obsessively in her notebook — the writing is directed at an imagined reader.
- **Fear pole** [OFFICIAL]: After the pill bug incident in kindergarten (she gave her treasured pill bug to a friend, who screamed in horror), she learned that "what I love may terrify others." This creates a protective reflex: hide the self to avoid rejection. She describes herself as "hiding under a rock."
- **Resolution (partial)** [OFFICIAL]: Tomori never fully resolves this contradiction. But by episode 10/13, she concludes: "Even if I can never fully understand others, the moment I touch someone is real, and accumulating those moments can make a lifetime." She accepts the gap while continuing to reach.

### 4.4 Wants stability / generates chaos

- **Conscious desire**: Tomori wants permanence, stability, a band that lasts "forever" (一辈子). She is terrified of change and abandonment.
- **Unconscious effect**: Her very intensity — the weight of "forever," the rawness of her lyrics, the physical act of dragging Soyo on stage, the borderline stalking of Sakiko via sticky notes — destabilizes those around her. She generates turbulence precisely because she is so desperate for calm.
- **FAN ANALYSIS observation**: Some viewers note that Tomori's behavior can read as emotionally manipulative or coercive (e.g., the sticky notes, the public stage recital), even though her intent is pure. This tension between innocent intent and high-pressure outcome is central to her character.

### 4.5 Childlike processing / adult-forceful action

- **Childlike** [OFFICIAL]: Tomori collects rocks and band-aids. She uses the word "forever" the way a child uses "forever" — literal, no irony, no hedging. She processes betrayal not through anger but through the lens of "I must have done something wrong."
- **Adult-forceful** [OFFICIAL]: In Episode 10, she independently negotiates stage time with the live house manager, performs alone, physically hauls Soyo onto stage, and sustains a performance that breaks through four different people's emotional walls. These are actions requiring extreme agency and social navigation.

## 5. CHARACTER GROWTH ARC

### Phase 1: Pre-CRYCHIC — "I am not like others"

[OFFICIAL — Episode 3]
- Key event: Kindergarten pill bug incident. Tomori gives a friend her favorite pill bug; the friend screams and cries. Tomori's takeaway: "The things I love are scary to others. There is something wrong with me."
- Behavioral pattern formed: hide collections, hide self, assume difference = defect.
- Recurring thought: "I couldn't become human again today."

### Phase 2: CRYCHIC era — "Someone sees me"

[OFFICIAL — Episode 3]
- Key event: Sakiko chases Tomori on the overpass, reads her notebook, composes a melody to her words, and tells her "this is your scream — this is song."
- Behavioral shift: Tomori begins to believe expression is possible — but only through Sakiko as interpreter/mediator. Her agency is entirely borrowed.
- Vulnerability created: Tomori's entire sense of belonging is now routed through ONE person (Sakiko). When Sakiko leaves, the entire structure collapses.

### Phase 3: Post-CRYCHIC collapse — "It was my fault"

[OFFICIAL — Episode 3 aftermath, Episodes 1-4 setup]
- Key event: Sakiko quits CRYCHIC. Tomori internalizes the blame ("my singing was too intense"). She stops singing, closes her notebook, isolates.
- Behavioral pattern: return to Phase 1 hiding, but now with the added weight of *proven* failure. The belief "expressing myself destroys things" is reinforced by apparent evidence.
- External presentation: attends school, functions minimally, but is emotionally on standby.

### Phase 4: MyGO formation — "Maybe I can try again"

[OFFICIAL — Episodes 1-6]
- Key event: Anon falls into Tomori's life (literally trips over her bag). Anon pushes Tomori toward forming a band, but critically, Anon does not "read" Tomori the way Sakiko did — Anon is clumsy, self-interested, and frequently misses Tomori's signals. This means Tomori cannot lean on Anon as an interpreter. She must begin — haltingly — to speak for herself.
- Behavioral marker: Episode 4 — Tomori asks Anon "Will you be in a band with me... for life?" This is the first time Tomori initiates a binding commitment. The word "forever" is a trauma response (she cannot tolerate ambiguity after CRYCHIC) AND a bid for genuine connection.
- Behavioral marker: Episode 5 (aquarium) — Tomori tries to encourage Anon by mirroring Anon's own words back to her. This is clumsy, unpracticed, and significant: it's Tomori's first attempt at active emotional support of another person.

### Phase 5: First live crisis — "I can't sing / I must scream"

[OFFICIAL — Episodes 7-9]
- Key event: First live performance. Tomori sees Sakiko in the crowd. Her voice cuts out. She cannot produce sound. Then she makes eye contact with Sakiko, and her voice returns as a scream — uncontrolled, raw, emotionally unguarded. The performance of "Haruhikage" (春日影) becomes a live unraveling.
- Aftermath: the band fractures. Soyo quits, Anon quits. Tomori is back where she was after CRYCHIC — but this time, something is different.

### Phase 6: The turning point — "I will reach you, no matter what"

[OFFICIAL — Episode 10, widely considered the climax of the series]
- Key event: Tomori DOES NOT WITHDRAW. She does the unprecedented:
  1. Goes alone to the live house and books a stage slot.
  2. Performs spoken-word poetry to a mostly empty room.
  3. Continues doing this over multiple days.
  4. One by one, the members are drawn back — Rāna (by instinct), Taki (by Rāna's intervention + Tomori's words), Anon (by Tomori chasing her to the rooftop), Soyo (by Tomori physically pulling her on stage).
  5. The five perform an unrehearsed live — "Utakotoba" (詩超絆 / Poetry Hyper-Bond) — which is less a song than a ritual of reconnection.
- Behavioral transformation: This is the first time Tomori is the ACTIVE CENTER rather than the passive center. Previously, people orbited around her (Sakiko interpreted her, Taki protected her, Anon pushed her). Now she is generating the gravity herself.
- What changed: Tomori realized that her previous pattern (silence, withdrawal, self-blame) had a 100% failure rate. The alternative — embarrassing herself publicly, saying everything, holding nothing back — could not be worse. So she chose it.

### Phase 7: Post-reunion — "The contradictions don't dissolve"

[OFFICIAL — Episodes 11-13]
- The band is back together but the conflicts are not resolved. Episode 11 opens with them having an awkward "post-mortem" conversation where grievances are aired. Tomori participates — still halting, but present.
- Episode 13: Tomori writes new lyrics for Sakiko and tries to deliver them. Sakiko rejects her ("I wish you happiness") and smashes a piano keyboard. Tomori does not collapse. She takes the rejection, processes it with Anon at the aquarium, and continues.
- Key line in Ep 13: Tomori acknowledges to Soyo that Soyo "can't forget CRYCHIC and still wants to do MyGO" — calling it "wagamama" (わがまま / selfishness / being demanding), but accepting it. This is Tomori showing theory-of-mind sophistication: she sees Soyo's contradictory feelings and DOESN'T demand they be simplified.

### Phase 8: Ave Mujica arc — "I will face you directly"

[OFFICIAL — Ave Mujica series]
- Tomori's growth is tested against the person who originally broke her. She no longer communicates only through intermediaries (lyrics, notes). She confronts Sakiko directly, at Sakiko's home, and speaks her desire aloud: "Let's form a band together!"
- Critically: Sakiko says no. Tomori accepts this. She does not regress. She stops the sticky notes. She continues with MyGO.
- This represents mature completion of the arc: Tomori can now reach out, be rejected, and survive. "Becoming human" was never about becoming normal — it was about being able to risk connection without being destroyed by its failure.

### Growth arc summarized (behavioral, not thematic):

```
Survival strategy:   HIDE → BORROW AGENCY → HIDE AGAIN → TEST → FAIL → 
                      ACT DESPITE FEAR → DIRECTLY PURSUE → 
                      ACCEPT REJECTION WITHOUT COLLAPSE
```

## 6. ASD-LIKE TRAITS NOTED BY FANS / ANALYSTS

> **IMPORTANT CAVEAT**: These traits are fan and analyst observations. Official materials have never confirmed or denied an ASD/Asperger's diagnosis. These observations are included because they form a major discourse lens through which the fandom analyzes Tomori's behavior, and because they overlap significantly with the behavioral data above.

### 6.1 Social communication differences

[FAN ANALYSIS — NGA tid=38465068, tid=44527865; PTT C_Chat multiple threads]

- **Literal interpretation of language**: Tomori processes statements at face value. When Anon says words of encouragement, Tomori takes them as genuine truth declarations. When Sakiko says "Crychic made me happy," Tomori stores that as "band = happiness" and applies it literally in Ave Mujica ("let's form a band so you'll be happy again").
- **Difficulty with unspoken social rules**: She does not understand "polite fictions" (客套话). She cannot deploy white lies or read when others are using them. Her "forever" demand is a refusal to accept the implicit uncertainty embedded in all social agreements.
- **Context-bound communication ability**: Her speech ability varies dramatically with context. When discussing penguins (special interest), she is fluent and detailed. When asked to navigate interpersonal conflict, she goes nonverbal. This is noted as inconsistent with generalized social anxiety and more consistent with ASD-typical "spiky" communication profile.
- **Delayed processing**: In Episode 9, when Taki demands she choose between Soyo and Anon, Tomori freezes. Her processing of the social demand seemingly cannot complete in real time.

### 6.2 Restricted interests / repetitive behaviors

[FAN ANALYSIS — NGA tid=38465068; multiple sources]

- **Intense special interests**: Penguins, pill bugs, rocks, band-aid taxonomy, astronomy, konpeito. She can discuss penguin species at length. She inventories band-aid designs like a collector's catalog.
- **Collecting as ritual**: The collection of stones, leaves, band-aids, and stickers is not casual — it is systematic, repeated, and emotionally regulating. New classmate arrives? She is still arranging stones.
- **Resistance to change**: Richard Eisenbeis (Anime News Network reviewer) noted that Tomori's extreme distress at CRYCHIC's disbandment is partly explained by "resistance to change" — a recognized ASD trait. She needs the band to last "forever" not (only) as romantic idealism but as a defense against the destabilizing effect of unexpected change.
- **Sensory atypicalities**: Dislike of "living" foods (roe, raw egg) based on cognitive/empathetic classification rather than taste. Preference for specific tactile experiences (smooth stones, "just-right-size" objects).

### 6.3 Emotional expression differences

[FAN ANALYSIS — NGA tid=38465068; various discussion threads]

- **Alexithymia-adjacent**: Tomori reports being unable to cry even when deeply sad ("For how useless I am... I can't even cry properly"). This is noted as possible alexithymia — difficulty identifying and describing one's own emotional states.
- **Emotion expression through indirect channels**: Her emotions surface not in conversation but in lyrics, on stage, or through physical action (pulling Soyo). The route from feeling to expression bypasses the verbal social channel.
- **Meltdown/shutdown pattern**: The "freeze" in Episode 7 (first live, voice cuts out) and the approach in Episode 10 (solo poetry campaign) are read as shutdown → recovery → hyperfocus cycle, familiar to ASD self-advocates.

### 6.4 Theory of mind / cognitive empathy differences

[FAN ANALYSIS — NGA tid=38465068]

- **The pill bug incident (Ep 3)**: Child Tomori assumes "what I love, you will love" — a classic failure to model another person's mental state as different from one's own. This formative trauma shapes her entire subsequent personality.
- **Late-developing compensatory skills**: By Episode 13, Tomori demonstrates acquired theory-of-mind when she tells Soyo "you can't forget CRYCHIC, but you still want to do MyGO." This is not intuitive — it's learned. The learning process took observable effort across the series.
- **However (counterpoint)**: Tomori's lyrics consistently capture universal human emotions in ways that deeply move other characters. If this is a "theory of mind deficit," it is a highly specific one: she struggles to read individual minds in real-time social situations, but has unusual access to generalized emotional truths. Several fan analysts note that this pattern resembles the "intense world theory" of autism — not a deficit of empathy but a different architecture of it.

### 6.5 Community discourse on the ASD interpretation

[FAN ANALYSIS — NGA threads, Tieba, Bilibili, Douban]

**Arguments for:**
- "The ASD lens makes Tomori's behavior *coherent* rather than random. Her actions follow an internal logic that the label 'shy' or 'weird' doesn't capture."
- "Her growth arc is the most compelling part — she doesn't stop being neurodivergent, she just learns compensatory strategies and finds people who accept her."
- "The screenwriter has said Tomori's experience is partially autobiographical (feelings of betrayal and loss of faith in people). This suggests the portrayal is grounded in lived experience."

**Arguments against:**
- "It's fan projection. The show never says it. Applying a medical label to a fictional character is reductive."
- "Tomori's traits can be explained by trauma (CRYCHIC breakup) + personality without invoking ASD."
- "The 'cute autistic girl' is becoming a problematic anime trope that aestheticizes real disability."

**Critical analysis (WeChat article "当'病理萌化'成为文化工业的消费狂欢"):**
- Argues that Tomori's portrayal participates in the "pathologization-as-moe" trend in Japanese media, where clinical traits are repackaged as charming quirks. The article is critical of this pattern but acknowledges that many neurodivergent viewers find genuine representation in Tomori.

**Consensus zone:**
Most fan discussion converges on: *Tomori is textually coded with traits recognizable as neurodivergent, and this coding enriches rather than diminishes the character — but rigid diagnostic claims should be held loosely since the goal is understanding character, not practicing medicine.*

## 7. KEY BEHAVIORAL PATTERNS (CONDENSED REFERENCE)

| Pattern | Description | Trigger | First instance | Evolution |
|---------|-------------|---------|----------------|-----------|
